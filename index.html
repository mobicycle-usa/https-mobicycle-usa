We can be forgiven for thinking of electronic waste as digital waste as opposed to a physical product.

Net Zero Electronics

A critical resource

Abandoned electronics contain precious metals. By some estimates, $62.5 billion-worth of value exists in discarded electronic items worldwide.

Unwanted

The term 'eWaste' refers to products that are unwanted, not working, and nearing or at the end of their useful life.

A pollutant

As the world's fastest growing waste stream, improperly discarded ewaste pollutes our air, soil, and water.

Do you work for a company, government, non-profit, manufacturer, retailer or in waste management? We help you sustainably manage electronics and electricals (EE).

Mining. There's been much discussion on renewables as a way to curb global warming. Unless we reclaim the minerals used to power renewables, we will mine for them. The mining of critical minerals poisons groundwater and devastates local ecosystems. The promotion of renewables in the absence of large scale recovery initiatives is greenwashing.

Manufacturing. The manufacturing process creates extremely potent greenhouse gases. SF6s and NF3s are much more damaging to the environment than CO2 or carbon dioxide.

Purchases and Sales. The Paris Agreement set a goal to limit warming to below 1.5 degrees Celsius. This goal is no longer feasible, but the guidance remains in place. The Greenhouse Gas Protocol specifically asks you to track emissions from purchases and sales (of electronic and electrical equipment).

Disposals. Electronic waste is the fastest growing waste stream in the world.Electronic waste degrades the natural defenses within our environment. When improperly treated, eWaste pollutes our air, soil and waterways. A weakened ecosystem has a negative impact on biodiversity. We need these defenses to fight climate change.
